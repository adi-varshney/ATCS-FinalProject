Real-World Examples

Founder effects have been studied in several isolated or historically separated populations. These examples show how population history can influence the frequency of inherited conditions.

HTT gene study

Lake Maracaibo, Venezuela

Gene involved: HTT

Some families in the Lake Maracaibo region have a high rate of Huntington's Disease. Researchers have studied this population to better understand how inherited diseases can become common in an isolated community.

EVC / EVC2 variants

Amish Communities

Genes involved: EVC and EVC2

Ellis-van Creveld Syndrome appears more often in some Amish communities because a small number of founders carried rare gene variants that were passed down through generations.

Remote island population

Tristan da Cunha

Condition: Retinitis Pigmentosa

Tristan da Cunha is a remote island community. Its small founder population and geographic isolation increased the frequency of some inherited conditions, including forms of retinitis pigmentosa.

BRCA founder mutations

Ashkenazi Jewish Populations

Genes involved: BRCA1 and BRCA2 founder mutations

Certain BRCA mutations occur at higher frequencies in Ashkenazi Jewish populations because of historical population bottlenecks and founder effects.
