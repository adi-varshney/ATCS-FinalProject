Genetics and population history

The Founder Effect in Isolated Populations

The Founder Effect happens when a small group separates from a larger population and starts a new population. Because the new group has only a sample of the original population's genes, some traits may become more common or less common by chance. This is one example of genetic drift, where allele frequencies change randomly over time. In isolated populations, limited migration and smaller population size can make unusual genetic traits appear more often.

Key Ideas

Genetic Drift

Genetic drift is a random change in the frequency of traits or alleles in a population. It has a stronger effect in small populations because chance events can influence the gene pool more.

Founder Effect

The Founder Effect is a type of genetic drift that begins when a new population is started by only a few individuals from a larger group.

Isolated Population

An isolated population has limited contact with other groups. Since fewer new genes enter the population, certain inherited traits can remain common for many generations.
